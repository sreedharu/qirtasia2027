01 / About

A thirty‑five
year tradition,
continued.

Since 1992, the Quantitative InfraRed Thermography conferences have convened specialists from industry and academia to present the field's most significant developments.

The QIRT‑Asia series, established in 2015, extends that tradition to the region's fast‑growing thermography community.

Over four days in Singapore, we bring together the researchers, engineers and practitioners shaping the next decade of thermal science — through technical sessions, keynote lectures, short courses, and a dedicated industry exhibition at Marina Bay Sands.

The programme is structured around four pillars: measurement science, applications, computational methods, and industry practice. Papers will be published in the conference proceedings and selected contributions will appear in a dedicated special issue.

Delegates are welcomed to Singapore — a crossroads of Asian research and innovation.

03 / Programme

Conference
topics.

Eight tracks spanning the breadth of quantitative infrared thermography — foundational, applied and emerging.

T/01

Active thermography

Lock‑in, pulsed, pulse‑phase and vibrothermography techniques for defect detection and material characterisation.

T/02

Passive thermography

Condition monitoring, predictive maintenance and in‑situ diagnostics across civil, mechanical and electrical systems.

T/03

Medical & bio-IR

Clinical thermography, early disease detection, inflammation mapping and veterinary applications.

T/04

Industrial NDT

Non‑destructive evaluation, weld inspection, quality control and process monitoring in manufacturing.

T/05

Aerospace & defence

Composite inspection, thermal signature analysis and engine diagnostics for aerospace and defence systems.

T/06

Building diagnostics

Energy audits, envelope performance, moisture detection and HVAC assessment using infrared imaging.

T/07

Cultural heritage

Non‑invasive study of paintings, murals and historical structures; conservation science and restoration.

T/08

AI & data methods

Machine learning, deep thermography, inverse problems and advanced signal processing for IR imagery.

04 / Keynote

Featured
speaker.

A distinguished programme led by a world-renowned figure in infrared thermography research.

Distinguished Keynote · 10 March 2027
Prof. Xavier
Maldague
Université Laval · Canada Research Chair in Multipolar Infrared Vision

Professor Maldague is among the most influential voices in contemporary infrared thermography, with pioneering contributions to active thermographic NDT, pulse‑phase methods, and the application of thermal imaging to aerospace, civil and cultural heritage domains. He has authored over 300 refereed publications and multiple textbooks foundational to the field.
